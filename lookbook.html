The LookbookSpring · Vol. I

A garden, quietly tended.

Editorial portraits in linen, silk & hand-finished embroidery.

Six frames, each a meditation on growth. The light is held a moment longer. The needle rests. The garment breathes.

“He hath made every thing beautiful in his time.” Ecclesiastes 3 · 11

01
Garden light

Where the morning finds her.

The opening frame — ivory linen and a hand of mist. The light is not posed; it is permitted. She turns her shoulder toward it the way one greets a friend.

Worn: L’Aube en Rose, No. 01, with the Petale slip beneath.

See the look in collection →
02
Sleeve study

A petal, opened slowly.

Two frames of a single sleeve — the way a rose discloses itself. Hand-pulled French knots in matte silk over hand-loomed organza. Each knot is its own small prayer.

Worn: Petale Blouse, No. 06.

03
Evening I

The hour of quiet ceremony.

A velvet cape in deep garnet, lined in blush silk. The body underneath remains soft; the surface answers the room. Worn open, it walks like water.

Worn: La Cape, Grenat, No. 16.

04
Rose brocade

The maison’s signature.

Brocade woven in rose and ink, finished with a single covered button at the nape. The back is left uncluttered — we believe a woman should be beautiful coming and going.

Worn: Rose Brocade Gown, No. 04.

05
The portrait

She is the centre of her own season.

One frame, held longer than the others. No motion, no veil. The dress is finished; the woman is unfinished — in the best way. Becoming, still. Bloom is a verb.

Worn: La Mariee, Aurore, No. 12.

06
Detail

What the close frame remembers.

A hand-stitched hem. A satin label. The small marks of the maker, kept where only the wearer knows. This is where the garment becomes hers.
