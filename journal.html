Letters from the maison.

Quiet writings from the atelier — on craft, faith, patience and the slow art of dressing. A page or two each month, when the needle pauses long enough to set it down.

Letter No. 07May, this year

A garment as a prayer.

A long letter on why we sit a long time with a single seam, and what the needle teaches the hand that holds it. On thread as devotion, and on the silence in which a piece becomes itself.

“The needle does not move quickly because it is slow. It moves quickly because it has been still a long time first.”
